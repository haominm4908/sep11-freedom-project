# Entry 6
##### 06/09/2026

#### Overview
As we head closer to the end of the year, the SEP class had to present their Freedom project in class and in the special event called the SEP EXPO. At the EXPO, we are greeted with two audiences: The judges, and the general audience. With the former being a group of individuals who will be grading how one presents their idea, and how well their final product looks. I had to present in class and in the EXPO, and there are some things I could improve on. 

#### Presentations
Overall presenting, I was able to maintain good eye contact and make sure I know who my audience is. What differred is how I projected my presentation between in class and how it was in the SEP expo. During class, I came in with unprepared [slides](https://tinyurl.com/2fn3vmwt) While the slides did have the barebones of what my [project](https://tinyurl.com/mr3v53fs) was, and its short highlights, but it lacked content and overall didn't present what the project was fully. When presenting in class, I wasn't able to project my voice loud enough, which was something I continue to work on. However, in the EXPO, time is scarce, and so we had to come up with an Elevator pitch, a brief overview of your project that highlights it's good qualities. I was able to project my voice loud enough and maintain eye contact with my audience to convey my elevator pitch. The elevator pitch was short and simple, ask a simple question to draw their curiosity, then give a short overview of my project and its highlights. Overall, it was a familiar experience, as I had done the presenting last year, but theres still more to work on. Maintaining confidence and making sure everything is prepared beforehand is a crucial takeaway I have learned from this experience.
